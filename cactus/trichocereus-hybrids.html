Trichocereus (hybrids)

Trichocereus hybrid cacti are also known as Echinopsis hybrids.

Taxonomy & identification

  • Trichocereus hybrid cacti are also known as Echinopsis hybrids. [Trichocereus Hybrids]
  • Trichocereus hybrid cacti are also known as Echinopsis hybrids. [Trichocereus Hybrids by Linda Hollingsworth, Bexar County Master Gardener]
  • Trichocereus hybrids are columnar in growth and can grow to about 4 feet high at maturity. [Trichocereus Hybrids by Linda Hollingsworth, Bexar County Master Gardener]

Growing & propagation

  • Most Trichocereus hybrids are self-sterile, so they are mostly a cross of two different parents. [Trichocereus Hybrids]
  • Most buds open from late night into early morning and last only one day. [Trichocereus Hybrids]
  • Recommended fertilization: 0-10-10 from mid-January until buds end; 10-10-5 once per month June through October; no fertilizer (rest) November through mid-January. [Trichocereus Hybrids]
  • They are tolerant of hard frosts (down to about 25 F / -4 C) and of heat. [Trichocereus Hybrids]
  • They can be grown in containers or in the ground, in filtered shade to full sun with afternoon shade, and do well under Palo Verde or mesquite trees. [Trichocereus Hybrids]
  • They are propagated by stem cuttings (allowed to callus over) or by pups (offsets). [Trichocereus Hybrids]
  • Cutting spent blooms off close to the stem promotes greater flowering rather than producing fruit from pollinated flowers. [Trichocereus Hybrids]
  • Trichocereus hybrids generally bloom in early May depending on weather and temperature, can bloom in many sets, and flower color varies with weather and fertilization. [Trichocereus Hybrids by Linda Hollingsworth, Bexar County Master Gardener]
  • Most buds open from late night into early morning and the blooms last only one day; the flowers attract bees, butterflies, and hummingbirds. [Trichocereus Hybrids by Linda Hollingsworth, Bexar County Master Gardener]
  • Recommended fertilization: 0-10-10 from mid-January until buds end; 10-10-5 once per month June through October; no fertilizer (rest period) November through mid-January. [Trichocereus Hybrids by Linda Hollingsworth, Bexar County Master Gardener]

Clone genealogy

  • Hybrid offspring tend to resemble the mother plant more closely, though there are exceptions. [Trichocereus Hybrids]
  • Trichocereus hybrids are mostly a cross of two different parents because most are self-sterile. [Trichocereus Hybrids by Linda Hollingsworth, Bexar County Master Gardener]
  • Hybrid offspring tend to come closer to the mother plant, although there are exceptions. [Trichocereus Hybrids by Linda Hollingsworth, Bexar County Master Gardener]
