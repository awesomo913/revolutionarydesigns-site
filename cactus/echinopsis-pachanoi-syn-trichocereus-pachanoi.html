Echinopsis pachanoi (syn. Trichocereus pachanoi)

Originally described as Trichocereus pachanoi by Britton & Rose (1920). In 2012 Albesiano & Kiesling reduced it to Trichocereus macrogonus var. pachanoi.

Taxonomy & identification

  • Originally described as Trichocereus pachanoi by Britton & Rose (1920). In 2012 Albesiano & Kiesling reduced it to Trichocereus macrogonus var. pachanoi. As Trichocereus is now treated as a synonym of Echinopsis, POWO (as of late 2025) lists Echinopsis pachanoi (Britton & Rose) H.Friedrich & G.D.Rowley as the accepted name and treats Trichocereus macrogonus var. pachanoi as a synonym. [https://powo.science.kew.org/taxon/88444-2; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Echinopsis_pachanoi]

Native range

  • The native range is Ecuador and Peru; the species is also found (naturalized/introduced) in Bolivia, Colombia, central Chile, the Canary Islands and mainland Spain, and is cultivated worldwide (per Wikipedia summarizing POWO data). [https://powo.science.kew.org/taxon/88444-2; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Echinopsis_pachanoi]

Habitat

  • It is a fast-growing columnar cactus of the Andes, occurring at roughly 2,000-3,000 m (6,600-9,800 ft) elevation, and grows primarily in the seasonally dry tropical biome. [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Echinopsis_pachanoi; https://powo.science.kew.org/taxon/88444-2]

Conservation

  • Echinopsis pachanoi is assessed as Least Concern (IUCN Red List Categories and Criteria v3.1) based on wide distribution; the use of the species is not considered a major threat partly because it is widely cultivated. The current entry is a 2017 amended version of a 2013 assessment (Ostolaza, Caceres & Roque). [https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Echinopsis_pachanoi]
  • As a member of the family Cactaceae, the species is covered by CITES Appendix II, meaning international trade in the plant and most of its parts/derivatives is regulated (with exemptions such as seeds, in vitro seedling/tissue cultures, and certain other categories per the CITES Cactaceae treatment). [https://www.kew.org/sites/default/files/2019-09/CITESandCacti_full.pdf; https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Echinopsis_pachanoi]
